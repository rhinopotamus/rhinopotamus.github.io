Admissible curves of the form given in (2.2.1) are called by other authors variations -- hence, the "calculus of variations". Think of them as starting with \(y(x)\) and "wiggling" it by \(\eta(x)\text{.}\) So, I'm going to define a wiggle as an admissible function \(\eta(x)\) that satisfies the conditions \(\eta(x_1) = \eta(x_2) = 0\text{.}\)
