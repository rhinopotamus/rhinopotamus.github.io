The fact that the equations (3.4.4) represent a cycloid of the kind described in the theorem is easily proved. For let a circle of radius \(b\) begin to roll on the line \(y=\alpha\) at the point whose co-ordinates are \((a, \alpha)\text{,}\) as shown in FigureÂ 3.5.1. After a turn through an angle of \(u\) radians the point of tangency is at a distance \(bu\) from \((a, \alpha)\text{,}\) and the point which was the lowest in the circle has rotated to the point \((x, y)\text{.}\) The values of \(x\) and \(y\) may now be readily calculated in terms of \(u\) from the figure, and they are found to be those given by equations (3.4.4).
