At every point \((x, y)\) of a field \(F\) the straight line of the field has a slope \(p(x, y)\text{,}\) the function so defined being called the slope-function of the field. The integral \(I^*\) (from equation (2.5.2)) with this slope-function in place of \(p\) in its integrand has a definite value along every arc \(C_{35}\) in the field having equations of the form (2.6.1), as we have seen on page 25. We can prove with the help of the formulas of the last section that the integral \(I^*\) associated in this way with a field has the two following useful properties:

Theorem 2.6.2.

  • The values of \(I^*\) are the same along all curves \(C_{35}\) in the field F having the same end-points 3 and 5.

  • Furthermore along each segment of one of the straight lines of the field the value of \(I^*\) is equal to the length of the segment.
