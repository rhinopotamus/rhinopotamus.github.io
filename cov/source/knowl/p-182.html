Another way you could rewrite \(I'(0)\) -- and the way the book does it -- is by messing with the first integral. Pretend it's not a definite integral for a hot second, and just use integration by parts to come up with an antiderivative. (Hint: Let \(u = \eta\text{,}\) and write down some reasonable thing for the antiderivative of \(dv\text{,}\) even though you don't know exactly what it is.)
