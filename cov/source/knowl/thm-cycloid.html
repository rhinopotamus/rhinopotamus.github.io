Theorem 3.4.2.

A curve down which a particle, started with the initial velocity \(v_1\) at the point 1, will fall in the shortest time to a second point 2 is necessarily an arc having equations of the form

\begin{equation} x-a = b(u + \sin u), \quad y-\alpha = b(1+\cos u).\tag{3.4.4} \end{equation}

These represent the locus of a point fixed on the circumference of a circle of radius \(b\) as the circle rolls on the lower side of the line

\begin{equation*} y = \alpha = y_1 - \frac{v_1^2}{2g}. \end{equation*}

Such a curve is called a cycloid.
