The mathematician who first made satisfactory sufficiency proofs in the calculus of variations was Weierstrass, and the ingenious device which he used in his proofs is called a field.

This is definitely not the same kind of field we study in abstract algebra.
For the problems which we are considering in this chapter a field \(F\) is a region of the \(xy\)-plane with which there is associated a one-parameter family of straight-line segments all of which intersect a fixed curve \(D\text{,}\) and which have the further property that through each point \((x, y)\) of \(F\) there passes one and but one of the segments.
