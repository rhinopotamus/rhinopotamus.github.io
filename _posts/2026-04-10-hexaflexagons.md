## Ingredience

You will need: 
- color printouts of the [jrmf hexa-hexaflexagon](https://jrmf.org/wp-content/uploads/Hexaflexagons-Festival-Guide.pdf)
- glue sticks 
    - (I mean it)
    - (If you use tape, your thing will be so thick that it won't flex nicely.)

## Instructions for assembly

### Stage 1: Make the long, double-thick strip

0. (If you are going to do any mapping stuff later, consider writing numbers on each of the colors. I find that the blue and the purple are often a bit hard to tell apart, for instance.)
1. Cut out the two parts of the strip.
    - On one end of each part, there's white triangles with a black border around them. You want to keep those attached; they are glue tabs for later.
    - On the other end of the strip, there's notches where there *aren't* borders. Cut those ones out.
    - Thus your two pieces will look like if Pac-Man was a very colorful dachshund[^1].

![I have cut the two parts out of the sheet of paper, showing the attached glue tabs and the removed notches](/images/hexa/montage1.jpg)

2. Fold both of the parts in half, hot-dog style, with the color sides out.
3. Glue the insides together.
    - Now your two parts are both double-thick.

![The two strips are shown folded in half, and I've applied glue to the inside of one](/images/hexa/montage2.jpg)

4. On one part, you have a blue triangle sticking out; on the other part, you have a yellow triangle sticking out. Glue the back of the blue triangle to the back of the yellow triangle.
    - Now you have created one long strip, which is double-thick all the way along!
    - On one side of this long strip, all the warm colors are happening. On the other side, all the cool colors are happening.

![Showing the two sticking-out triangles that will be glued together, and demonstrating the long strip that results](/images/hexa/montage3.jpg)

### Stage 2: Fold up the hexaflexagon

5. Starting with the warm colors, fold same-colored triangles diagonally onto each other: yellow onto yellow, orange onto orange, and red onto red.
    - Here's a picture of folding red onto red. Notice the blue dashed line I drew where I am making a valley fold. 
    ![A creased strip with a blue dashed line down the border between two red triangles](/images/hexa/red-onto-red.jpg)

    - Your strip will kinda coil up as you do this. Here's a partially folded strip.
    ![A partially folded strip that has begun to coil](/images/hexa/coiled.jpg)

6. Once all the warm color diamonds are folded up, now all the cool color triangles will be lined up in diamonds. Fold blue onto blue three times.
    - In the picture below I am pointing out the first two blue-onto-blue folds; once these two folds are made, only green and purple will be visible. Flip the strip over to find the third blue diamond.
    
![All the warm colors have been folded, leaving only cool colors visible](/images/hexa/montage4.jpg)

7. Now your gadget will be a hexagon with one green side and one purple side. Hooray!

![A hexagon with one green side and one purple side](/images/hexa/montage5.jpg)


8. The white triangles with black borders are glue tabs. Tuck the one triangle behind the other so that the glue tabs line up, and then glue them together.

![Showing how the glue tabs look after they have been tucked but before they have been glued](/images/hexa/tucked.jpg)

[^1]: A word I have needed to look up how it is spelled every time I have ever attempted to write it.
